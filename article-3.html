Real-World Asset Tokenisation Crosses $10B in On-Chain AUM

By the DeltaBoxx Research Team  ·  9 min read

The tokenisation of real-world assets has crossed a milestone that few would have predicted with confidence even two years ago. As of Q1 2025, the total value of tokenised assets — spanning private credit, real estate, treasury funds, commodities, and private equity stakes — held on public blockchains has surpassed $10 billion. This figure, while modest relative to the multi-trillion dollar pools of traditional private assets, represents a proof-of-concept at institutional scale and the beginning of what many believe will be one of the most significant transformations in capital markets infrastructure in a generation.

The $10 billion headline masks considerable heterogeneity. Tokenised US Treasury products — on-chain representations of short-duration government debt yielding money-market returns with blockchain-native settlement — account for the largest single category, driven by demand from DeFi protocols and crypto-native treasuries seeking yield without custody risk. Tokenised private credit occupies the next tier, with platforms enabling fractional participation in senior secured loans to SMEs and real estate borrowers at ticket sizes previously inaccessible to individual accredited investors. And tokenised real estate — both commercial property and residential mortgage pools — is growing rapidly as platforms build compliant infrastructure for cross-border property investment without the friction of traditional fund structures.

Why Now?

Three developments converged to make this moment possible. First, the regulatory environment clarified. The EU's MiCA framework provided the first comprehensive pan-jurisdictional ruleset for digital asset issuance and custody. The SEC's updated guidance on digital asset securities, while still imperfect, gave institutional participants enough clarity to proceed. And regulators in Singapore, the UAE, and the UK published frameworks specifically designed to facilitate tokenised fund structures, creating competitive jurisdictional options for issuers.

Second, blockchain infrastructure matured sufficiently to meet institutional-grade requirements. Earlier tokenisation attempts foundered on the rocks of inadequate throughput, unpredictable gas costs, and immature custody solutions. The emergence of purpose-built institutional blockchain rails — high-throughput, low-cost, with programmable compliance logic baked into the protocol — resolved many of these concerns. Ethereum's transition to proof-of-stake reduced energy concerns. Layer-2 networks dramatically cut transaction costs. And regulated digital asset custodians, including subsidiaries of major global banks, now offer insured, audited custody for tokenised securities.

Third, the demand side crystallised. Asset managers with trillions under management — BlackRock, Franklin Templeton, JPMorgan's Onyx, and others — launched tokenised products and demonstrated that institutional AUM could flow into on-chain vehicles without legal or operational incident. This first-mover credibility unlocked a second wave of manager participation that is still gathering momentum.

The Private Credit Opportunity

Within the RWA universe, tokenised private credit is the category DeltaBoxx watches most closely, given its direct intersection with our investment mandate. The traditional private credit market — worth approximately $1.7 trillion globally as of year-end 2024 — has historically been accessible only to investors with $5 million or more to deploy and the operational capacity to manage complex subscription documents, capital call schedules, and illiquidity periods measured in years. Tokenisation is beginning to dissolve these barriers.

Platforms operating in this space are enabling accredited investors to participate in diversified private credit portfolios with minimum tickets as low as $10,000, with monthly liquidity windows enabled by secondary market matching engines. For borrowers, tokenised credit can reduce origination costs and expand the investor base, potentially compressing borrowing spreads. For the ecosystem, on-chain private credit creates a new asset class for DeFi protocols seeking yield-bearing collateral that is not correlated with cryptocurrency price volatility.

The risks are real and should not be minimised. Smart contract risk, oracle manipulation, regulatory reclassification, and the fundamental challenge of enforcing off-chain legal rights through on-chain mechanisms are all live concerns. The most credible platforms address these through rigorous legal structuring — typically involving a bankruptcy-remote special purpose vehicle holding the underlying assets, with tokens representing beneficial interests in that SPV governed by familiar legal frameworks in established jurisdictions.

Real Estate Tokenisation: Fractional Ownership at Scale

Real estate is arguably the asset class with the most to gain from tokenisation, given the extraordinary illiquidity, high transaction costs, and large minimum investment sizes that characterise traditional property investment. A tokenised commercial real estate vehicle can enable an investor in Singapore to hold a fractional interest in a logistics warehouse in Frankfurt, receive pro-rata rental income in near-real-time, and sell their position on a secondary market — all without engaging a fund administrator, legal counsel, or foreign bank account.

Several platforms have demonstrated this model at meaningful scale. Commercial real estate tokens issued on compliant blockchain infrastructure have attracted capital from geographically diverse investor bases that traditional real estate funds would have found operationally impossible to service. Tokenised residential mortgage-backed securities — a category that requires extraordinary legal precision given the lessons of 2008 — are also emerging from regulated issuers who have invested in the compliance infrastructure to do it properly.

The Road to $1 Trillion

Boston Consulting Group estimates that tokenised asset AUM could reach $16 trillion by 2030. Even discounting for typical consultancy optimism, the structural forces driving tokenisation adoption — regulatory clarity, infrastructure maturity, institutional validation, and genuine investor demand for lower-friction access to alternative assets — suggest a multi-year growth trajectory that will make $10 billion look like a rounding error in retrospect.

DeltaBoxx's position at the intersection of TradFi and Web3 means we are both a participant in and a beneficiary of this transition. We are actively evaluating tokenised structures for elements of our own portfolio and monitoring the regulatory and technical developments that will determine the pace and shape of mainstream adoption. The $10 billion milestone is not the destination — it is the proof of concept that makes the next hundred billion possible.
