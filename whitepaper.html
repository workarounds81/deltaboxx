Section 02

The Opportunity

Two converging waves

Two of the largest capital-formation waves in a generation are arriving at the same time. The first is the maturation of a cohort of Pre-IPO AI, robotics, and deep-tech companies that have reached commercial scale and are approaching public listings — where the steepest part of the value-creation curve happens before the bell ever rings. The second is the migration of traditional financial assets onto programmable, on-chain rails through real-world asset tokenization.

Access is the bottleneck

The best Pre-IPO allocations are stratified — they go first to the venture funds who led earlier rounds, then to sovereign wealth and large institutions, and only then to a select group of strategic investors. Most private investors enter late, at inflated valuations, with no governance rights. The information asymmetry is profound: public market investors get audited filings and analyst coverage; private investors work from teaser decks and limited management access.

Where DeltaBoxx fits

DeltaBoxx exists to close that gap. We combine institutional-grade diligence, a founder and operator network built over years, and pre-secured allocation relationships to source opportunities that would otherwise be unreachable — then we structure them cleanly so aligned capital can participate on the same terms we do.

Section 03

The DeltaBoxx Thesis

Every technology wave has a hidden structure: first the infrastructure is built, then the applications arrive, and finally the mainstream follows. The internet needed fibre, TCP/IP, and HTTP before Google or Amazon were possible. The AI era needs compute, robotics platforms, and agentic operating systems before its defining applications can scale.

"We do not chase narratives. We back the infrastructure those narratives will be built on."

Why infrastructure wins

  • Network effects compound at the base layer. Once a platform reaches adoption — an agentic OS, a payment network — switching costs make displacement extremely difficult.
  • Value accrues to primitives, not interfaces. Interfaces commoditise; the underlying platform captures the durable economics.
  • Pre-IPO entry is asymmetric. Disciplined entry valuations ahead of a listing offer materially better risk-adjusted upside than buying on the open market post-IPO.
  • Tokenization expands the surface. RWA rails make previously illiquid private assets transferable, composable, and accessible.

Our edge

DeltaBoxx's edge is not simply capital — it is the combination of deep sector diligence, a network of founders and operators that gives us primary information beyond what is in any data room, and the relationships to secure allocation in oversubscribed rounds. We invest where we have genuine informational and relational advantage, and we hold with conviction through to a defined exit.

Section 05

Instruments & Structuring

We use established, clean legal instruments matched to each opportunity. Investor funds are never commingled — every deal carries its own structure and distribution waterfall.

SPV

Special Purpose Vehicle

Single-deal vehicle that pools investor capital into a Pre-IPO equity allocation — as used for LimX Dynamics — with a defined exit at IPO or secondary sale.

S.A.F.E

Simple Agreement for Future Equity

Converts to shares at a future priced round or liquidity event. Used for our Lightnet Series A position, projected at 3–4× on shares at exit.

S.A.F.T

Sale of Future Tokens

Rights to tokens delivered at network launch, liquid from listing. Used for our Velo and Evry positions, which returned 8× at initial token release.

Section 07

The DBXX Token

DBXX is DeltaBoxx's native utility and governance token — the mechanism through which the firm extends its investment thesis to a broader base of aligned participants. It is not a speculative asset; it is a programmatic claim on participation, governance, and access.

DBXX grants governance rights over capital allocation, profit participation from realised portfolio exits, and preferential access to new deal allocations.

  • Governance: Holders vote on portfolio direction, strategy, and treasury decisions — fully on-chain.
  • Profit participation: A defined share of realised gains from exits is distributed to stakers via smart contract.
  • Allocation rights: Tiered holders receive priority access to new deals on the same terms as DeltaBoxx.

Section 09

Risk Factors

Private market and digital asset investments carry meaningful risk. Prospective participants should carefully consider the following before engaging with DeltaBoxx.

  • Illiquidity: Pre-IPO and S.A.F.E positions are illiquid until a qualifying event, and there is no guarantee one occurs on a predictable timeline. Post-IPO lock-ups may apply.
  • Market volatility: Token and equity valuations can decline materially in short timeframes. Past performance is not indicative of future results.
  • Regulatory risk: The legal landscape for digital assets and private offerings is evolving across jurisdictions and may affect structuring, distribution, or exits.
  • Execution & technology risk: Portfolio companies may fail to execute; token networks and smart contracts may contain vulnerabilities despite audits.
  • Concentration risk: A concentrated book means underperformance by a small number of holdings can disproportionately affect returns.

This is not an exhaustive list. Participants are strongly encouraged to conduct independent due diligence and seek qualified legal and financial advice before making any investment decision.
