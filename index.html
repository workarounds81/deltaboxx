Common Questions

FAQ

DeltaBoxx sits at the intersection of traditional private equity, Web3, and AI. In practice that means three deal types: equity instruments in high-growth private companies (S.A.F.E and Series rounds), token instruments in digital asset ecosystems (S.A.F.T — Sale of Future Tokens), and AI-driven infrastructure plays. Past completed deals include a $3M Series A S.A.F.E in Lightnet, a Southeast Asian cross-border payments company, and $5M across the Velo ecosystem via S.A.F.T — $4M in Velo token and $1M in Evry token.
A S.A.F.E (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) is a contract that converts into equity shares when a company hits a defined milestone — typically a priced funding round or an IPO. Our Lightnet position is structured this way, with shares projected to return 3–4× at exit. A S.A.F.T (Sale of Future Tokens) is the token equivalent — investors fund a project in exchange for the right to receive tokens once the network launches. The Velo and Evry tokens delivered 8× upon initial release, demonstrating the upside potential of early-stage token allocation at the right entry point.
Access is restricted to accredited and institutional investors. Log in via the Investor Portal to view open allocations, deal materials, and your current position summary. If you are not yet a registered investor, reach out through our contact channel to begin the onboarding process. Each deal is structured on its own terms and minimum ticket sizes vary by instrument.
For equity positions (S.A.F.E), returns are realised at a liquidity event — a priced Series round, secondary sale, or IPO — at which point the S.A.F.E converts to shares and investors receive proceeds per the agreement. For token positions (S.A.F.T), tokens are distributed directly to investors upon network launch and are liquid from the point of listing. DeltaBoxx does not commingle investor funds; each deal has its own clean legal structure and distribution waterfall.
Private equity and early-stage token investments carry meaningful risk. S.A.F.E instruments are illiquid until a qualifying event and there is no guarantee one occurs on a predictable timeline. Token investments are subject to market volatility, regulatory developments, and protocol execution risk. Past performance — including the 8× Velo return and projected Lightnet upside — is not a guarantee of future results. DeltaBoxx mitigates risk through rigorous due diligence, legal structuring via established instruments, and concentration in deals where we have informational and relational edge.
